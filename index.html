About Me

I am an IoT and Robotics Engineering student with strong hands-on experience in designing embedded systems that address real-world challenges in healthcare monitoring, assistive technologies, environmental sensing, and smart automation.

My engineering philosophy emphasizes system reliability, measurable performance, and deployment-readiness rather than proof-of-concept prototypes. I actively focus on building systems that can operate under real constraints such as limited power, low-cost hardware, noisy sensor data, and unstable network conditions.

Technically, my work spans the full stack of IoT and embedded system development — starting from schematic design and PCB prototyping, moving through embedded firmware development on ESP32 microcontrollers, integrating digital and analog sensors, implementing MQTT-based communication pipelines, and deploying machine learning models for edge-level intelligence.

I have authored and presented peer-reviewed research at IEEE conferences, demonstrating my ability to conduct applied research, analyze experimental data, and translate academic concepts into working engineering systems.

I am currently seeking international research opportunities, scholarships, and remote engineering roles where I can contribute to technically challenging projects with meaningful social impact.
