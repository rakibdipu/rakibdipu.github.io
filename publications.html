IoT-Based Real-Time Patient Monitoring System with Predictive Analytics

IEEE RAAICON 2025 March 2025 Conference Paper
Your Name, Co-Author Name, Supervisor Name

Abstract

This paper presents a cost-effective IoT-based patient monitoring system designed for resource-limited healthcare facilities. The system integrates ESP32 microcontroller with MAX30102 and DHT22 sensors to continuously monitor heart rate, blood oxygen saturation, and body temperature. Data is transmitted via MQTT protocol to a cloud-based dashboard for real-time visualization. We implemented a Random Forest machine learning model achieving 94% accuracy in predicting critical health events 3-5 minutes before conventional threshold-based alarms. The complete system costs $35 per unit and demonstrates scalability for multi-patient monitoring networks.

Keywords: Internet of Things, Healthcare Automation, ESP32, Machine Learning, MQTT, Patient Monitoring
