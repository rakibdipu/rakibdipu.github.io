Lightweight IoT-Driven Panic Attack Detection and Caregiver Alert System for Autism Spectrum Disorder

IEEE RAAICON 2025 November 2025 Conference Paper
MD. Rakib Hassan Dipu, Co-authors

Abstract

This paper presents a lightweight, cost-effective IoT-driven system for early detection of panic attacks and automated caregiver alerting in individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). The proposed system integrates an ESP32-based embedded platform with physiological and environmental sensors to enable continuous real-time monitoring.

Sensor data is transmitted using the MQTT protocol to a cloud-connected dashboard for visualization and alert management. A machine learning pipeline based on Random Forest classification was developed to predict panic-attack indicators, achieving approximately 94% accuracy and enabling detection 3–5 minutes before conventional threshold-based alarms. The complete system was designed with a per-unit cost under USD $40, making it suitable for deployment in resource-constrained environments.

Keywords: Internet of Things, Healthcare Automation, Autism Care, ESP32, Machine Learning, MQTT, Embedded Systems
Conference: 4th IEEE International Conference on Robotics, Automation, Artificial Intelligence, and Internet-of-Things (RAAICON 2025)
Location: Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST), Dhaka, Bangladesh
